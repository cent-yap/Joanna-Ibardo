From Games to Grades

Joanna Ibardo

Childhood Years

1. Here’s my childhood story. 2 I loved playing outside with my childhood friends. 3 We played Chinese garter, piko, tagu-taguan, and many other Filipino games. 4 It was always fun, and we never got tired of running around. 5 We laughed, shouted, and competed with each other every day. 6 No matter how hot or rainy it was, we always found a way to play. 7 I still remember when it was getting darker outside, it would be time to go home. 8 But I never wanted to stop playing. 9 Every evening, my lolo would come looking for me. 10 He would call me angrily, “Uwi na, Kababae mong tao, nasa labas ka pa!” 11 The moment I saw him, I would quickly run back home. 12 At that time, I felt mad whenever I was forced to stop playing. 13 I thought it was unfair that I had to go home while my friends could still play. 14 I would stomp my feet and pout, wishing I could stay outside longer. 15 But now that I am grown up, I understand why my lolo was so strict. 16 He was only protecting me and making sure I was safe. 17 Looking back, those childhood moments make me smile. 18 I miss the days when life was simple, and all I cared about was playing. 19 The friendships I made during those years were priceless. 20 Those memories will always stay with me.

Teenage Years

1. When I was in my teenage years, around 7th to 10th grade, my classmates and I would hang out together after school. 2 We would go to KTVs or the mall, just to play in the arcades, because we had free time to do whatever we wanted. 3 After playing, we would go out to eat and enjoy each other’s company. 4 Those were some of the best moments of my teenage years. 5 We had so much fun. 6 My teenage years were also filled with school competitions, such as dancing and role-playing. 7 I was very outgoing back then, never shy to join any activity. 8 I loved being involved in everything, whether it was dancing, acting, or just having fun with friends. 9 Now, I can’t imagine doing those things anymore. 10 My confidence during my teenage years was something I took for granted, but now I feel different. 11 I also joined the varsity badminton team in high school. 12 We competed in intramurals, playing against different schools. 13 I participated in both singles and doubles events. 14 Sometimes we won gold, sometimes silver, and those wins made me proud. 15 I worked hard with my teammates, and the experiences taught me a lot about teamwork. 16 Looking back, I miss the energy and excitement of those days. 17 I miss the competitions, the fun with friends, and the freedom to do whatever I wanted. 18 Now, I feel like I can’t do the same things I used to do. 19 My teenage years were full of unforgettable moments that I will always remember. 20 I learned a lot during those years, and they shaped the person I am today.

College Years

1. Now that I am in college, my first year was hard. 2 I had trouble adjusting to the new setup. 3 Sometimes I felt overwhelmed and wondered if I was doing things right. 4 I often questioned if I chose the right course. 5 There were times when I had breakdowns because of the pressure. 6 It was difficult at first, but it has become a bit easier. 7 I met some classmates who became my support system. 8 They helped me whenever I needed it. 9 I also learned how to manage my time better. 10 I now know how to balance schoolwork and free time. 11 College is different from high school, and I can’t do the same things I used to. 12 I also miss going to arcades with friends, but I still try. 13 I’ve learned how to organize my time so I don’t get too stressed. 14 I try to stay focused on my studies and make time for fun. 15 Being in college has taught me a lot. 16 It’s not easy, but I’m learning how to handle it. 17 I still sometimes feel pressure, but I know how to deal with it now. 18 I’ve grown a lot since I started college. 19 Even though it’s tough, I know I’m getting better at it. 20 My college years have been challenging, but I’m making it through.
